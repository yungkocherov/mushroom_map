Mushroom keys (distinctive features):
- porcini: white-stemmed bolete with thick bulbous stem (often with white
    netting pattern), cream / yellow pores under cap (no gills), and a
    LIGHT-toned cap: pale tan, cream-brown, light hazel, light olive-brown.
- pine_bolete: same shape and white-netted stem as porcini, but with a
    DARK-toned cap: chestnut, reddish-brown, mahogany, dark-brown,
    rust-brown. «Боровик» / «колосовик». Don't require near-black —
    a clearly dark-chestnut or mahogany cap is enough.
- TIE-BREAK porcini vs pine_bolete: judge by dominant cap tone of the
    visible mushrooms. If a basket / pile mixes light AND dark caps
    (typical mixed haul), split into BOTH entries with proportional
    counts — do not lump everything into one.
- aspen_bolete: bolete with a STEM covered in dark / black scales (this
    is the diagnostic — porcini and pine_bolete never have black scales,
    they have white netting). Cap color spans bright orange, red-orange,
    brick-red, rusty-brown, and yellow-orange (the «красноголовик» /
    L. versipelle form). If you can clearly see black scales on a white
    stem, choose aspen_bolete even when the cap leans brown.
- birch_bolete: grey-brown smooth cap (NOT orange, NOT red), stem with
    dark scales like aspen_bolete but cap is muted brown / grey-brown.
- mokhovik: small-to-medium bolete, velvety olive / yellow-brown cap (often
    cracked into pattern), bright yellow pores that bruise blue, slim stem
- chanterelle: ANY chanterelle / funnel mushroom — covers all variants:
    · classic chanterelle (bright golden-yellow, trumpet / vase shape)
    · trumpet chanterelle (small thin funnel, grey-brown outside, yellow
      inside, HOLLOW stem, autumn, grows in clusters in moss)
    · horn of plenty / вороночник (dark grey-black funnel, thin flesh)
    Key signs: funnel / trumpet / vase shape, ribbed (NOT gilled) underside,
    false gills running down the stem. Use one «chanterelle» entry summing
    ALL variants visible.
- saffron_milkcap: orange cap with concentric rings, orange milk
- white_milkcap: white cap with wavy / fringed edges, white milk
- woolly_milkcap: pink cap with concentric zones, hairy edge
- spring_mushroom: wrinkled honeycomb / brain-like cap (spring only)
- honey_fungus: clusters of small tan caps on wood / stumps
- oyster: large shell / fan caps sideways on tree trunk, no central stem
- russula: brittle flat cap, bright color (red/yellow/green/violet), WHITE
    stem, NO ring, NO white spots on cap
- fly_agaric: BRIGHT RED cap with WHITE WART-LIKE SPOTS, white stem with
    a ring (skirt) and a bulbous base. The white spots distinguish it from
    russula. If cap is red but spotless → russula, not fly_agaric.

Berry keys (only if clearly visible as the main subject):
- blueberry: small round dark blue / purple-black berries (черника)
- cloudberry: orange / amber raspberry-shaped berries on low plants (морошка)
- cranberry: small round bright red berries on moss (клюква)

- other: any mushroom that's clearly visible but doesn't match any key above

count = TOTAL of that species across the WHOLE photo, summed across all
baskets / piles. Full basket ≈ 30-50, half ≈ 15-25, handful ≈ 5-10.
