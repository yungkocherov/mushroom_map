List ONLY items you can clearly identify. Don't guess from texture/color
alone. Typical photo has 2-4 species — listing 8+ usually means guessing.
One entry per species.

Mushroom keys (distinctive features):
- porcini: white-stemmed bolete, thick bulbous stem (often with white
    netting), cream / yellow pores under cap (no gills), LIGHT cap —
    pale tan, cream-brown, light hazel, or light olive-brown.
- pine_bolete: same shape and white-netted stem as porcini, but with a
    DARK cap: chestnut, mahogany, dark-brown. Clearly dark is enough —
    does not need to be near-black. NOT orange, NOT red, NOT rust-toned
    — those are aspen_bolete, not pine_bolete.
- TIE-BREAK porcini vs pine_bolete: judge by dominant cap tone. If a
    basket / pile mixes light AND dark caps, split into BOTH entries
    with proportional counts.
- aspen_bolete: Leccinum-type bolete — white stem with DARK / BLACK
    SCALES, AND an orange / red cap. The combination is diagnostic.
    Cap colors: bright orange, red-orange, brick-red, yellow-orange.
    If you see dark scales on a white stem together with a clearly
    orange or red cap, call aspen_bolete — do not downgrade to
    pine_bolete on the basis of cap tone.
- birch_bolete: Leccinum-type bolete with the same dark-scaled white
    stem as aspen_bolete, BUT the cap is PLAIN BROWN or GREY-BROWN —
    no red, no orange. Cap palette: pale beige, grey-brown, tan,
    mushroom-brown, medium brown, dark brown. Smooth domed cap, not
    velvety, not cracked.
- mokhovik: small-to-medium bolete, velvety olive / yellow-brown cap
    (often cracked), bright yellow pores that bruise blue, slim stem.
- chanterelle: ANY chanterelle / funnel mushroom — funnel / trumpet /
    vase shape with ribbed (NOT gilled) underside and false gills
    running down the stem. Variants: classic (bright golden-yellow),
    trumpet (small grey-brown funnel, yellow inside, hollow stem),
    horn of plenty (dark grey-black). One entry summing all.
- saffron_milkcap: orange cap with concentric rings, orange milk
- white_milkcap: white cap with wavy / fringed edges, white milk
- woolly_milkcap: pink cap with concentric zones, hairy edge
- spring_mushroom: ANY morel, verpa, or gyromitra. DIAGNOSTIC: the cap
    is NOT a smooth disc — it is wrinkled, pitted, lobed, brain-like,
    saddle-shaped, or honeycomb-textured.
      · morel — honeycomb cap with deep pits, brown, hollow, fused to stem
      · verpa — small brown wrinkled thimble cap hanging FREE from a tall
        pale HOLLOW stem. Baskets look like pale stems topped with little
        brown caps — do NOT call those brown caps porcini.
      · gyromitra — brain-like or saddle lobes, dark reddish-brown,
        no honeycomb
- honey_fungus: clusters of small-to-medium tan caps with a ring on the
    stem, growing on wood / stumps. Two visual variants — both go here:
      · autumn type — yellow-brown cap with small darker scales,
        slightly larger
      · summer type — smaller, smooth two-tone cap (darker centre
        fading to lighter edge, water-marked look), no scales
- oyster: large shell / fan caps sideways on tree trunk, no central stem
- russula: brittle flat cap (smooth disc), bright color (red / yellow /
    green / violet), WHITE stem, NO ring, NO white spots on cap.
    NOT russula: small bright-red CUP / bowl with no real stem sitting
    on ground or wood (a spring cup-fungus) → use «other».
- fly_agaric: BRIGHT RED cap with WHITE WART-LIKE SPOTS, white stem
    with a ring and a bulbous base. The white spots distinguish it
    from russula. If cap is red but spotless → russula, not fly_agaric.

Berry keys (only if clearly visible as the main subject):
- blueberry: small round dark blue / purple-black berries
- cloudberry: orange / amber raspberry-shaped berries on low plants
- cranberry: small round bright red berries on moss

- other: any mushroom that's clearly visible but doesn't match any key

count = TOTAL of that species across the WHOLE photo, summed across all
baskets / piles. Full basket ≈ 30-50, half ≈ 15-25, handful ≈ 5-10.
